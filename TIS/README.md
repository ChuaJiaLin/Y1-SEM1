# Reflection

The Information Systems course provided valuable knowledge and a foundational understanding of technology and information systems, including key areas such as networks, databases, hardware, and software. The course established a clear overview of how different technological components work together to support organizational processes and decision-making.

Throughout the course, theoretical concepts were effectively linked with practical applications, enhancing critical thinking and problem-solving skills. Activities such as the design thinking report encouraged the analysis of real-world problems and the development of innovative, technology-driven solutions. This approach strengthened the ability to evaluate issues systematically and propose effective strategies.

In addition, the course contributed to the development of communication and teamwork skills. Collaborative tasks and project-based activities promoted active participation, idea sharing, and coordination among team members, which are essential in completing academic and professional projects successfully.

The course also increased awareness of how information systems are applied in real-world environments to improve efficiency, decision-making, and organizational performance. This highlighted the importance of integrating technical knowledge with practical skills in addressing modern technological challenges.

Overall, the course successfully enhanced both theoretical understanding and practical competency in information systems, while strengthening critical thinking, problem-solving, communication, and teamwork skills essential for future academic and professional development.
